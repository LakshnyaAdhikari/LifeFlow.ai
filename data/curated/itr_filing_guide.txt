INCOME TAX RETURN FILING IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Domain: Taxation
Authority: Income Tax Department / CBDT
Source: Compiled from Income Tax Act 1961, CBDT Notifications, Income Tax Portal Help

=== WHICH ITR FORM TO FILE ===

| ITR Form | Who Should File |
|---|---|
| ITR-1 (Sahaj) | Salaried individuals with income up to Rs 50 lakh. Sources: Salary, one house property, other income (interest, dividend), agricultural income up to Rs 5,000 |
| ITR-2 | Individuals/HUFs with income from capital gains, more than one house property, foreign assets, income above Rs 50 lakh, or not eligible for ITR-1 |
| ITR-3 | Individuals/HUFs having income from business or profession |
| ITR-4 (Sugam) | Individuals/HUFs/Firms with presumptive income under Sections 44AD/44ADA/44AE. Turnover up to Rs 3 crore |
| ITR-5 | Partnership firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs |
| ITR-6 | Companies (other than those claiming Section 11 exemption) |
| ITR-7 | Trusts, political parties, universities, scientific research institutions |

=== HOW TO FILE ITR ONLINE ===

STEP 1: Login to incometax.gov.in with PAN and password
STEP 2: Go to e-File → Income Tax Returns → File Income Tax Return
STEP 3: Select Assessment Year (AY 2025-26 for FY 2024-25)
STEP 4: Choose filing type: Original / Revised
STEP 5: Choose ITR form (the portal auto-suggests based on your profile)
STEP 6: Fill or import pre-filled data (Form 16, AIS/TIS, 26AS)
STEP 7: Verify all income details, deductions, and tax computations
STEP 8: Pay any remaining tax due (via challan 280)
STEP 9: Preview and submit the return
STEP 10: e-Verify within 30 days (Aadhaar OTP / Net banking / DSC)

=== KEY DEADLINES ===

| Category | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Individual (no audit) | July 31 of Assessment Year |
| Business (audit required) | October 31 of Assessment Year |
| Transfer pricing cases | November 30 of Assessment Year |
| Revised return | December 31 of Assessment Year |
| Belated return | December 31 of Assessment Year (with penalty) |

LATE FILING PENALTY:
- Before December 31: Rs 5,000
- After December 31: Rs 10,000
- If income below Rs 5 lakh: Maximum Rs 1,000

=== TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE (TDS) ===

TDS is tax deducted by the payer before making payment. Key TDS rates:

| Section | Payment Type | Threshold | TDS Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 192 | Salary | As per slabs | Slab rate |
| 194A | Interest (bank FD) | Rs 40,000/year | 10% |
| 194B | Lottery/game winnings | Rs 10,000 | 30% |
| 194C | Contractor payments | Rs 30,000 (single) / Rs 1 lakh (aggregate) | 1-2% |
| 194H | Commission/Brokerage | Rs 15,000 | 5% |
| 194I | Rent | Rs 2,40,000/year | 10% (land/building) |
| 194J | Professional fees | Rs 30,000 | 10% |
| 194N | Cash withdrawal | Rs 1 crore | 2% |

HOW TO CHECK TDS:
1. Login to incometax.gov.in
2. Go to e-File → View Filed Returns → View 26AS
3. 26AS shows all TDS deducted against your PAN
4. AIS (Annual Information Statement) shows comprehensive financial data
5. TIS (Taxpayer Information Summary) shows derived information

TDS REFUND:
- If TDS deducted exceeds actual tax liability, file ITR to claim refund
- Refund processed within 20-45 days of ITR processing
- Interest on refund: 6% per annum (for delay beyond 1 month)

=== INCOME TAX REFUND PROCESS ===

HOW TO CHECK REFUND STATUS:
1. Login to incometax.gov.in → e-File → Income Tax Returns → View Filed Returns
2. Or visit: tin.tin.nsdl.com/oltas/refund-status
3. Enter PAN and select Assessment Year
4. Refund status: Sent to bank / Adjusted against demand / Under process

COMMON REASONS FOR REFUND DELAY:
1. Bank account not validated/pre-validated on income tax portal
2. Mismatch between ITR and 26AS/AIS data
3. ITR not e-verified
4. Defective return (needs correction)
5. Outstanding tax demand adjusted against refund

HOW TO FIX:
- Pre-validate bank account on incometax.gov.in → Profile → My Bank Accounts
- Ensure PAN is linked to bank account
- File revised return if errors found

=== RESPONDING TO INCOME TAX NOTICES ===

COMMON NOTICES AND HOW TO RESPOND:

SECTION 139(9) — DEFECTIVE RETURN:
- Respond within 15 days
- Correct the defects and re-submit
- Login → e-File → e-Proceedings → Find and respond

SECTION 143(1) — INTIMATION:
- Processed return summary. Check if there's demand or refund
- If demand: Verify against your records, pay or file objection
- Response deadline: 30 days

SECTION 148 — REASSESSMENT:
- Income escaped assessment. Serious notice
- Must file return of income in response
- Get professional help (CA)
- Response deadline: 30 days (extendable)

SECTION 245 — ADJUSTMENT AGAINST REFUND:
- Department adjusting refund against past demand
- If you disagree: File response within 30 days

=== GST BASICS ===

GST REGISTRATION:
- Mandatory if turnover exceeds Rs 40 lakh (goods) / Rs 20 lakh (services)
- Apply at gst.gov.in using Form GST REG-01
- PAN-based registration
- Typically issued within 7 working days
- GSTIN: 15-digit unique identification number

GST RETURN FILING:
| Return | Due Date | Who |
|---|---|---|
| GSTR-1 (Outward supplies) | 11th of next month | All registered |
| GSTR-3B (Summary return) | 20th of next month | All registered |
| GSTR-9 (Annual return) | December 31 | Turnover > Rs 2 crore |
| GSTR-4 (Composition) | April 30 | Composition dealers |

GST REFUND:
- Apply via gst.gov.in → Refunds → Application for Refund
- Types: Excess payment, export refund, inverted duty structure
- Processed within 60 days

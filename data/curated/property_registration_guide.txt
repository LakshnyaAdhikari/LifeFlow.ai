PROPERTY REGISTRATION AND LAND RECORDS IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Domain: Property
Authority: Department of Land Resources / State Revenue Departments / RERA / NHB
Source: Compiled from Registration Act 1908, Transfer of Property Act 1882, RERA Act 2016, NGDRS Portal

=== PROPERTY REGISTRATION PROCESS ===

Under the Registration Act, 1908, all transfers of immovable property above Rs 100 must be registered.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS:

1. PROPERTY VERIFICATION:
   - Verify title deed and chain of ownership (last 30 years minimum)
   - Check encumbrance certificate from Sub-Registrar office
   - Verify property tax receipts are current
   - Check approved building plan and occupancy certificate
   - For agricultural land: verify land records (7/12 extract or equivalent)

2. AGREEMENT PREPARATION:
   - Draft sale deed/agreement through a lawyer
   - Include: seller/buyer details, property description, survey number, area, boundaries, price
   - Two witnesses required
   - Stamp duty payment on stamp paper (varies by state, typically 5-7% of property value)

3. STAMP DUTY PAYMENT:
   - Pay before or on the date of registration
   - Payment modes: e-stamping (shcilestamp.com), physical stamp paper, or online via NGDRS
   - Rates vary significantly by state and property type:

   | State | Residential | Commercial |
   |---|---|---|
   | Maharashtra | 5% (Mumbai 6%) | 6% |
   | Karnataka | 5% | 5% |
   | Tamil Nadu | 7% | 7% |
   | Delhi | 4-6% (based on gender) | 6% |
   | UP | 7% | 7% |
   | Rajasthan | 5-6% | 5-6% |

   - Women buyers often get 1-2% concession
   - Registration fee: 1% of property value (in addition to stamp duty)

4. DOCUMENT SUBMISSION AT SUB-REGISTRAR:
   - Book appointment online via NGDRS portal or state portal
   - Both buyer and seller must be present (or Power of Attorney holder)
   - Submit: Sale deed (original + copies), ID proofs, address proofs, property documents
   - Biometric verification (Aadhaar-based in most states)
   - Photographs taken at office
   - Registered deed returned within 30 minutes to 7 days

5. MUTATION IN REVENUE RECORDS:
   - After registration, apply for mutation (name change in land records)
   - Apply at local municipal corporation or Revenue department
   - Online portals available in most states
   - Fee: Rs 25-500
   - Timeline: 30-90 days

=== NGDRS — NATIONAL GENERIC DOCUMENT REGISTRATION SYSTEM ===

The NGDRS (ngdrs.gov.in) is a centralized platform for property registration currently operational in multiple states.

FEATURES:
- Online appointment booking
- Document submission and tracking
- e-Stamping integration
- e-KYC verification
- Property valuation guidance
- Registration status tracking
- Digital signed registered document download

STATES ON NGDRS (as of 2024):
Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Jharkhand, Goa, Tripura, Mizoram, Manipur, and others

=== LAND RECORDS DIGITIZATION (DILRMP) ===

The Digital India Land Records Modernization Programme aims to:
1. Computerize all land records
2. Survey/re-survey of land
3. Registration of deeds and documents
4. Integration of textual and spatial data (maps)

HOW TO CHECK LAND RECORDS ONLINE:
- Visit bhulekh portal of your state:
  - UP: upbhulekh.gov.in
  - Maharashtra: bhulekh.mahabhumi.gov.in
  - Karnataka: landrecords.karnataka.gov.in
  - Rajasthan: apnakhata.raj.nic.in
  - MP: mpbhulekh.gov.in

INFORMATION AVAILABLE:
- Khasra/Khatauni number
- Owner name and details
- Land area and classification
- Survey number and plot number
- Mutation history
- Court case status (if any)
- Encumbrance details

=== RERA — REAL ESTATE REGULATORY AUTHORITY ===

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) protects homebuyers.

KEY PROVISIONS:
1. All projects above 500 sq.m or 8 apartments must be registered with RERA
2. Builder must deposit 70% of buyer's money in separate escrow account
3. No pre-launch sales allowed — only after RERA registration
4. Carpet area must be clearly mentioned (not super built-up area)
5. Builder must deliver possession within timeline promised
6. If delayed, builder must pay interest to buyer at SBI prime + 2%
7. Structural defect warranty: 5 years from possession
8. Builder cannot change building plan without 2/3rd buyer consent

HOW TO FILE RERA COMPLAINT:
1. Visit your state's RERA portal (e.g., rera.maharashtra.gov.in)
2. File complaint online against registered project
3. Fee: Rs 1,000 (individual) to Rs 5,000 (association)
4. Adjudicating officer must decide within 60 days
5. Appeal to RERA Appellate Tribunal within 60 days
6. Further appeal to High Court within 60 days

DOCUMENTS NEEDED FOR RERA COMPLAINT:
- Builder-buyer agreement
- Payment receipts
- Correspondence with builder
- RERA registration number of project
- Allotment letter
- Any advertisement/brochure of the project

=== HOME LOAN GUIDELINES ===

RBI GUIDELINES FOR HOME LOANS:
- LTV Ratio: Up to 75-90% depending on loan amount
  - Up to Rs 30 lakh: Max 90% LTV
  - Rs 30-75 lakh: Max 80% LTV
  - Above Rs 75 lakh: Max 75% LTV
- Prepayment: No prepayment charges for floating rate loans (as per RBI)
- Processing fee: 0.25-1% of loan amount
- Age limit: Typically 18-65 years
- Maximum tenure: 30 years

PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS YOJANA (PMAY):
- Subsidy on home loan interest for first-time homebuyers
- EWS/LIG (income up to Rs 6 lakh): 6.5% subsidy on Rs 6 lakh loan for 20 years
- MIG-I (Rs 6-12 lakh): 4% subsidy on Rs 9 lakh loan for 20 years
- MIG-II (Rs 12-18 lakh): 3% subsidy on Rs 12 lakh loan for 20 years
- Apply through banks/HFCs
- Check eligibility at pmaymis.gov.in

HOME LOAN GRIEVANCE:
- Contact bank's nodal officer first
- If unresolved: File complaint at NHB (nhb.org.in)
- Alternatively: RBI Ombudsman (cms.rbi.org.in)

=== PROPERTY TAX ===

- Payable annually to local municipal corporation
- Based on property type, area, location, usage
- Typically 0.5-2% of property value per year
- Payment: Online via municipal corporation portal
- Exemptions available for: Self-occupied residential property (in some states), agricultural land, religious/charitable properties

=== IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS FOR PROPERTY TRANSACTIONS ===

1. Sale Deed (registered)
2. Title Deed / Mother Deed (proves ownership chain)
3. Encumbrance Certificate (proves no loans/legal issues)
4. Property Tax Receipts (proves taxes are paid)
5. Approved Building Plan
6. Occupancy Certificate / Completion Certificate
7. Society/Association NOC (for resale in apartments)
8. Power of Attorney (if applicable)
9. Khata Certificate (in some states)
10. 7/12 Extract (for agricultural/revenue land)

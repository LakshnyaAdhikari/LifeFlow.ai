VEHICLE AND TRANSPORT SERVICES IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Domain: Vehicle & Transport
Authority: Ministry of Road Transport and Highways / Parivahan / State RTOs
Source: Compiled from Motor Vehicles Act 1988 (Amended 2019), Parivahan Portal, Sarathi Portal

=== DRIVING LICENCE (DL) ===

TYPES OF LICENCES:
1. Learner's Licence (LL): Valid for 6 months. Can be applied for from age 16 (without gear, up to 50cc) or 18 (with gear).
2. Permanent Driving Licence: Applied after 30 days of getting LL. Valid for 20 years or until age 50 (whichever is earlier), then renewed every 5 years.
3. International Driving Permit (IDP): Valid for 1 year. Required for driving in other countries.

HOW TO APPLY FOR LEARNER'S LICENCE:
1. Visit Sarathi portal: sarathi.parivahan.gov.in
2. Select your state
3. Click "Apply for Learner's Licence"
4. Fill Form 1 (Medical Certificate) — get from registered medical practitioner
5. Fill Form 2 (Application for LL)
6. Upload documents: Proof of age, proof of address, passport size photos
7. Pay fee online: Rs 200 (LL application) + Rs 50 (LL test fee)
8. Schedule appointment at RTO
9. Pass LL test (written/computer-based MCQ test on traffic rules)
10. LL issued instantly on passing

HOW TO APPLY FOR PERMANENT DL:
1. Must have held LL for at least 30 days (not more than 180 days)
2. Visit Sarathi portal and apply for DL
3. Fill Form 4 (Application for DL)
4. Schedule driving test at RTO
5. Pay fee: Rs 200 (DL test fee) + Rs 200 (DL issuance)
6. Pass driving test at RTO
7. Smart card DL issued and mailed to address

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Learner's Licence
- Proof of age (birth certificate, school certificate, passport)
- Proof of address (Aadhaar, passport, utility bill)
- Medical certificate (Form 1) for transport vehicle DL
- Passport size photographs

RENEWAL OF DL:
- Apply within 1 year before or after expiry
- Apply online via Sarathi portal or in person at RTO
- No driving test required for renewal (unless DL expired more than 5 years ago)
- Fee: Rs 200 for renewal
- If applied late (more than 1 year after expiry): additional late fee and may need to retake test

=== VEHICLE REGISTRATION (RC) ===

All motor vehicles must be registered under Section 39 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

HOW TO REGISTER A NEW VEHICLE:
1. Dealer provides temporary registration (valid 1 month)
2. Apply at local RTO within 7 days of purchase
3. Submit Form 20 (Application for Registration) with:
   - Sale certificate from dealer
   - Insurance certificate
   - PUC (Pollution Under Control) certificate
   - Proof of address
   - PAN card or Form 60/61
   - Invoice and road tax receipt
4. Vehicle inspection at RTO
5. Registration certificate issued (RC smart card mailed)

REGISTRATION FEE:
- Two-wheeler: Rs 300-600 (varies by state)
- Four-wheeler: Rs 600-1500 (varies by state)
- Road tax: 4-20% of vehicle cost (varies significantly by state)

RC VALIDITY:
- Private vehicles: 15 years initially, then renewable every 5 years
- Commercial vehicles: 10 years initially, then renewable every 5 years
- Electric vehicles: Exemptions available in many states

=== TRANSFER OF VEHICLE OWNERSHIP ===

When selling/buying a used vehicle:

SELLER'S STEPS:
1. File Form 29 (Notice of Transfer of Ownership) at RTO within 14 days
2. Hand over RC, insurance certificate, PUC to buyer
3. Sign transfer documents (Form 29 and Form 30)

BUYER'S STEPS:
1. File Form 30 (Application for Transfer of Ownership) at RTO within 30 days
2. Submit: Form 29 (signed by seller), Form 30, RC, insurance, address proof, PAN
3. Pay transfer fee: Rs 300-1000 (varies by state)
4. If transferring between states: NOC from original RTO needed
5. Re-registration in new state within 12 months

=== E-CHALLAN — TRAFFIC FINES ===

Under the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019, penalties were significantly increased:

| Offense | Fine |
|---|---|
| Driving without licence | Rs 5,000 |
| Driving without insurance | Rs 2,000 (and/or 3 months imprisonment) |
| Over-speeding | Rs 1,000 - Rs 2,000 |
| Dangerous driving | Rs 5,000 (and/or imprisonment up to 1 year) |
| Drunk driving | Rs 10,000 (and/or imprisonment up to 6 months) |
| Driving without seatbelt | Rs 1,000 |
| Using mobile phone while driving | Rs 5,000 |
| Not giving way to emergency vehicles | Rs 10,000 |
| Driving without helmet (two-wheeler) | Rs 1,000 + 3-month suspension of DL |
| Juvenile driving | Rs 25,000 + 3 years imprisonment for guardian |

HOW TO PAY E-CHALLAN:
1. Visit: echallan.parivahan.gov.in
2. Enter challan number / vehicle number / DL number
3. View pending challans
4. Pay online via UPI, debit card, credit card, net banking
5. Download payment receipt

HOW TO CONTEST E-CHALLAN:
1. Visit the designated authority (Traffic Police headquarters)
2. File your representation with supporting evidence
3. Adjudication officer will hear your case within 60 days

=== VEHICLE FITNESS CERTIFICATE ===

Required for commercial vehicles (and private vehicles older than 15 years from 2024 rules):
- Commercial vehicles: Valid for 2 years
- Private vehicles (over 15 years): Valid for 5 years

HOW TO APPLY:
1. Book appointment on Vahan portal (vahan.parivahan.gov.in)
2. Take vehicle to Automated Testing Station (ATS) or RTO
3. Tests: braking efficiency, emission levels, lights, horn, wipers, tyre condition
4. Fee: Rs 200-400 (varies by vehicle type)
5. Fitness certificate issued if vehicle passes all tests

=== PERMIT FOR COMMERCIAL VEHICLES ===

Types of permits:
1. Contract Carriage Permit: For buses, taxis (within state)
2. Stage Carriage Permit: For route buses
3. Goods Carriage Permit: For goods vehicles
4. National Permit: For interstate goods transport
5. All India Tourist Permit: For tourist vehicles

HOW TO APPLY:
1. Apply online via Vahan portal
2. Submit Form 48/46 (Application for Permit)
3. Required documents: RC, fitness certificate, insurance, tax receipts
4. Fee: Rs 5,000-25,000 depending on type and state

=== MOTOR VEHICLES ACT 2019 AMENDMENT KEY CHANGES ===

1. Hit and Run compensation increased from Rs 25,000 to Rs 2 lakh (death) / Rs 50,000 (injury)
2. Good Samaritan protection: No civil or criminal liability for helping accident victims
3. Motor Vehicle Accident Fund for cashless treatment at golden hour
4. Electronic enforcement of traffic rules
5. Vehicle recall provision added for manufacturing defects
6. Central Motor Vehicles Rules amended to allow automated driving test

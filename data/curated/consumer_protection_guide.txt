CONSUMER PROTECTION IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Domain: Consumer Protection
Authority: Government of India / CCPA / NCDRC
Source: Compiled from Consumer Protection Act 2019, CCPA Guidelines, NCH Portal

=== THE CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019 ===

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (CPA 2019) replaced the older Consumer Protection Act, 1986. It received Presidential assent on August 9, 2019 and came into force on July 20, 2020.

KEY DEFINITIONS:
- Consumer: Any person who buys goods or hires/avails services for consideration (including online and electronic transactions). Does NOT include a person who buys goods for resale or commercial use.
- Complaint: Any allegation made by a consumer regarding unfair trade practice, defective goods, deficient service, excessive pricing, or offering hazardous goods/services.
- E-commerce: Buying or selling goods or services including digital products over a digital or electronic network.
- Product Liability: The responsibility of a product manufacturer, service provider, or seller to compensate a consumer for any harm caused by a defective product or deficient service.

=== CONSUMER RIGHTS (Section 2(9)) ===

1. Right to Safety: Protection against goods and services that are hazardous to life and property.
2. Right to be Informed: Access to information about quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, and price of goods or services.
3. Right to Choose: Access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices.
4. Right to be Heard: Consumer interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums.
5. Right to Seek Redressal: Right to fair settlement of genuine grievances.
6. Right to Consumer Education: Right to acquire knowledge and skill to be an informed consumer.

=== HOW TO FILE A CONSUMER COMPLAINT ===

STEP 1: Try to resolve directly with the seller/service provider. Keep all receipts, bills, and correspondence.

STEP 2: File complaint at National Consumer Helpline (NCH)
- Call 1800-11-4000 (toll-free) or 1915
- Online: consumerhelpline.gov.in
- WhatsApp: Send "Hi" to 8800001915
- The NCH will attempt mediation with the company

STEP 3: If unresolved, file at Consumer Commission
- District Commission: Claims up to Rs 1 crore
- State Commission: Claims Rs 1 crore to Rs 10 crore
- National Commission (NCDRC): Claims above Rs 10 crore
- Online filing portal: edaakhil.nic.in (E-Daakhil)

DOCUMENTS NEEDED:
- Written complaint with details of the transaction
- Copy of bill/receipt/invoice
- Copy of warranty/guarantee card (if applicable)
- Communication with the opposite party
- Any evidence supporting the complaint
- Details of relief/compensation sought
- Filing fee (varies by claim amount)

FILING FEES:
- Up to Rs 5 lakh: Rs 200
- Rs 5 lakh to Rs 10 lakh: Rs 400
- Rs 10 lakh to Rs 20 lakh: Rs 600
- Rs 20 lakh to Rs 50 lakh: Rs 1,000
- Rs 50 lakh to Rs 1 crore: Rs 2,000
- Rs 1 crore to Rs 2 crore: Rs 2,500
- Rs 2 crore to Rs 5 crore: Rs 3,000
- Rs 5 crore to Rs 10 crore: Rs 5,000
- Above Rs 10 crore: Rs 7,500

TIMELINE: Consumer Commissions must resolve complaints within 3 months (if no testing required) or 5 months (if testing required).

=== CENTRAL CONSUMER PROTECTION AUTHORITY (CCPA) ===

The CCPA was established under Section 10 of the CPA 2019 to protect consumer rights as a class.

POWERS OF CCPA:
1. Investigate and take action against unfair trade practices
2. Issue safety notices for goods/services
3. Order recall of dangerous goods or withdrawal of hazardous services
4. File class action suits
5. Pass orders for reimbursement of prices paid and discontinuation of unfair practices

MISLEADING ADVERTISEMENTS:
- CCPA can impose penalty of up to Rs 10 lakh on manufacturer/endorser for first offense
- Up to Rs 50 lakh for subsequent offenses
- Imprisonment up to 2 years for subsequent offenses
- Endorser who makes false endorsement can be fined Rs 10 lakh to Rs 50 lakh

=== E-COMMERCE CONSUMER PROTECTION ===

Under the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020:
1. E-commerce entities must display total price including taxes/fees
2. Must have a customer care mechanism with officer designated for complaint resolution
3. Must display details of registered office, customer care contacts
4. Cannot manipulate prices to gain unreasonable profit
5. Must have a clear cancellation and refund policy
6. Consumers can file complaints for goods bought online at their own location's Consumer Commission

=== PRODUCT LIABILITY (Chapter VI, Sections 82-87) ===

Manufacturer is liable if:
- Product contains manufacturing defect
- Product is defective in design
- Product deviates from specifications
- Product does not conform to express warranty
- Product lacks adequate instructions/warning

Service Provider is liable if:
- Service is faulty, imperfect, or deficient
- Service provider acted negligently
- Service does not conform to express warranty

Product Seller is liable if:
- Exercised substantial control over design/testing
- Altered or modified the product
- Made express warranty independent of manufacturer
- Did not exercise reasonable care in assembly/inspection

=== REMEDIES AVAILABLE ===

Consumer Commissions can order:
1. Removal of defects from goods
2. Replacement of goods
3. Refund of price paid
4. Payment of compensation for loss or injury
5. Discontinuation of unfair/restrictive trade practice
6. Adequate costs to parties
7. Punitive damages in appropriate cases

=== APPEAL PROCESS ===

- District Commission order → Appeal to State Commission within 45 days
- State Commission order → Appeal to National Commission within 30 days
- National Commission order → Appeal to Supreme Court within 30 days
- Deposit required: 50% of awarded amount or Rs 25,000, whichever is less

INSURANCE POLICYHOLDER RIGHTS AND CLAIMS IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Domain: Insurance
Authority: Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI)
Source: Compiled from IRDAI Protection of Policyholders Regulations 2024, Insurance Act 1938, IRDAI Master Circulars

=== POLICYHOLDER RIGHTS UNDER IRDAI REGULATIONS 2024 ===

The IRDAI (Protection of Policyholders' Interests, Operations and Allied Matters of Insurers) Regulations, 2024 came into force on April 1, 2024.

KEY RIGHTS:
1. RIGHT TO INFORMATION: Insurer must provide complete product information, terms and conditions, premium details, and claim process before purchase.
2. RIGHT TO FREE-LOOK PERIOD: 30 days from receipt of policy to return it (15 days for offline purchase). Full refund minus proportionate risk premium and medical expenses.
3. RIGHT TO POLICY IN PREFERRED LANGUAGE: Policy document must be available in language chosen by policyholder.
4. RIGHT TO CLAIM SETTLEMENT: Insurer must settle or reject claim within 30 days of receipt of last necessary document.
5. RIGHT TO GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL: Must have designated grievance officer. Must resolve in 15 days.
6. RIGHT TO PORTABILITY: Switch health insurance to another insurer without losing accumulated benefits.

=== HEALTH INSURANCE CLAIM PROCESS ===

CASHLESS CLAIM:
1. Get admitted to network hospital
2. Hospital sends pre-authorization to TPA/insurer
3. Insurer approves/rejects within 1 hour (emergency) or 6 hours (planned)
4. Treatment happens — hospital settles directly with insurer
5. Policyholder pays only deductible/co-pay/non-covered items

REIMBURSEMENT CLAIM:
1. Get treated at any hospital
2. Collect all original bills, discharge summary, prescriptions
3. Submit claim form with documents to insurer within 15-30 days
4. Insurer processes and settles within 30 days
5. Payment via NEFT/cheque

DOCUMENTS NEEDED FOR HEALTH CLAIM:
- Claim form (duly filled and signed)
- Hospital discharge summary
- All original hospital bills and receipts
- Doctor's prescriptions
- Investigation reports (blood tests, X-rays, scans)
- Pre-authorization form (for cashless)
- FIR copy (for accident cases)
- KYC documents of policyholder
- Policy copy
- Bank details for reimbursement

=== MOTOR INSURANCE CLAIM PROCESS ===

OWN DAMAGE CLAIM:
1. Inform insurer within 24 hours (for theft: FIR within 24 hours)
2. Do not repair vehicle before survey
3. Insurer appoints surveyor within 72 hours
4. Surveyor inspects damage and submits report
5. Take vehicle to network garage (cashless) or get repair done (reimbursement)
6. Submit repair bills, photos, FIR (if applicable)
7. Claim settled within 30 days of survey

THIRD PARTY CLAIM:
1. Under Section 166 of Motor Vehicles Act
2. File claim with Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT)
3. No upper limit on compensation (Third Party liability is unlimited)
4. Insurer pays compensation as per MACT order
5. Compensation includes: Medical expenses, loss of income, pain and suffering, future loss

=== LIFE INSURANCE CLAIM PROCESS ===

DEATH CLAIM:
1. Nominee/legal heir intimates insurer about death
2. Submit: Claim form, death certificate, policy bond, claimant's ID, bank details
3. If death within first 3 years: Insurer may investigate (Section 45 of Insurance Act)
4. If death after 3 years: Policy cannot be questioned on any ground
5. Insurer must settle within 30 days of receiving all documents
6. If claim rejected, written reasons must be provided

MATURITY/SURVIVAL CLAIM:
1. Insurer sends intimation 3 months before maturity
2. Submit: Discharge form, policy bond, ID proof, bank details
3. Settlement within 30 days of due date

=== INSURANCE OMBUDSMAN — COMPLAINT PROCESS ===

If insurer does not resolve your complaint within 30 days:

STEP 1: File complaint with insurer's Grievance Redressal Officer (in writing/email)
STEP 2: Wait 15 days for resolution
STEP 3: If unresolved, approach Insurance Ombudsman

HOW TO FILE WITH OMBUDSMAN:
1. Visit: ecoi.co.in (Council of Insurance Ombudsmen portal)
2. Or visit the Ombudsman office in your jurisdiction (17 offices across India)
3. File Form VI-A (available on the IGMS portal)
4. Claim amount should not exceed Rs 50 lakh
5. Complaint must be within 1 year of insurer's final response
6. Ombudsman's recommendation is binding on insurer if accepted by complainant
7. Decision within 3 months

ALTERNATIVELY — IRDAI IGMS PORTAL:
1. Visit: igms.irda.gov.in
2. Register complaint online
3. IRDAI forwards complaint to insurer
4. Insurer must respond within 15 days
5. Track complaint status online

=== BIMA BHAROSA PORTAL ===

Launched by IRDAI for policyholder protection:
- URL: bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in
- Features: Policy comparison, claim status tracking, complaint filing
- Can search for any insurance policy issued in your name
- View all policies across all insurers (linked to PAN/Aadhaar)

=== KEY IRDAI REGULATIONS CONSUMERS SHOULD KNOW ===

1. POLICY LAPSE AND REVIVAL: If premium unpaid for 30 days (grace period), policy lapses. Can be revived within 3-5 years by paying all dues + interest.

2. ASSIGNMENT AND NOMINATION: Policyholder can assign policy or change nominee anytime. Nomination is not an assignment — legal heirs may still have claims.

3. SURRENDER VALUE: Available after 3 years of premium payment (for traditional policies). Must disclose guaranteed and non-guaranteed surrender values at point of sale.

4. COOLING-OFF PERIOD: 15-30 days to review and cancel any insurance policy (full refund minus proportionate risk premium).

5. CLAIM SETTLEMENT RATIO (CSR): Insurer must publish CSR annually. Higher CSR = better claim settlement track record.

6. UNIT LINKED INSURANCE PLANS (ULIPs): 5-year lock-in period. Charges (mortality, fund management, policy admin) must be disclosed upfront.
